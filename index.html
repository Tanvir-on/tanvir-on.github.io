AgriSpectro: Affordable Spectroscopic Device for Non-destructive Fruit Quality Analysis

Recent advancements in non-destructive evaluation techniques, particularly Visible and Near-Infrared (Vis/NIR) Spectroscopy, have emerged as powerful tools for assessing internal fruit quality. These methods enable accurate measurement of parameters such as sugar content, acidity, firmness, and oil concentration without causing any physical damage to the produce.

This project seeks to develop a cost-effective, portable Vis/NIR device capable of rapid, non-destructive fruit quality assessment. The work encompasses the design and fabrication of the device, systematic collection of spectral data, and the application of machine learning algorithms to predict internal fruit attributes. Ultimately, the initiative aims to empower smallholder farmers with actionable insights for optimal harvest timing and market decisions, thereby minimizing postharvest losses, enhancing productivity, and improving overall profitability.

  • Reducing postharvest losses, enhancing productivity, and improving profitability.

Prediction of Early Blight Disease in Potato using Machine Learning

Potato (Solanum tuberosum L.) is one of the most important crops in Bangladesh but is highly vulnerable to Early Blight, a fungal disease that significantly reduces yield and quality. Disease development is strongly influenced by environmental conditions such as temperature, humidity, and wind speed. For instance, higher temperatures (35–38°C), lower evening humidity (30–58%), and moderate wind (1.2–2.2 km/h) have been shown to increase disease severity, while prolonged periods of high relative humidity (>90% for 5–6 hours) accelerate spore germination. Leveraging weather and microclimatic data provides an opportunity to predict disease outbreaks and enable timely interventions, helping farmers minimize losses and maintain crop health. This system leverages machine learning algorithms to analyze weather patterns and predict disease outbreaks, enabling proactive management strategies.

Our project develops an AI-powered early warning system for predicting Early Blight in potatoes using real-time microclimatic data from a portable mini weather station. The system provides farmers with timely, data-driven insights for precise disease management, reducing crop losses, minimizing fungicide use, and supporting sustainable agricultural practices across diverse farming conditions in Bangladesh.

  • Facilitates data-driven, precise disease management, helping farmers reduce losses and optimize fungicide usage.
  • Provides a practical and scalable solution for diverse farming conditions, enhancing potato crop health, productivity, and sustainability.

Smart Agriculture Robot: An Arduino-Powered Platform for Automated Soil Monitoring and Irrigation Control

The “Smart Agriculture Robot” is an Arduino-based automated platform designed to monitor soil conditions and control irrigation efficiently. Equipped with sensors to measure soil moisture, temperature, and other key parameters, the system makes real-time decisions to optimize water usage and maintain optimal crop growth conditions. By automating routine agricultural tasks, this platform reduces labor, conserves water resources, and enhances overall farm productivity. Its modular and cost-effective design makes it suitable for small to medium-scale farms, promoting sustainable and precision agriculture practices.

  • Demonstrate automation and manual control coexistence.
  • Minimizes the need for farmers to constantly check soil conditions, saving time and labor.

BARI-Based Crop Suggestion System: Soil and Season-Specific Recommendations for Optimal Yield

This project develops a data-driven Crop Suggestion System based on BARI (Bangladesh Agricultural Research Institute) guidelines, providing soil and season-specific crop recommendations for farmers. By analyzing key soil parameters such as pH, nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), along with seasonal conditions, the system identifies the most suitable crops for cultivation. The platform aims to enhance agricultural productivity, optimize resource utilization, and support smallholder farmers in making informed planting decisions. By integrating local research data with user-friendly recommendations, it promotes sustainable and efficient farming practices across Bangladesh.

  • Provides crop recommendations tailored to seasonal conditions for maximum yield.
  • Helps farmers plan cultivation effectively, reducing trial-and-error losses.
